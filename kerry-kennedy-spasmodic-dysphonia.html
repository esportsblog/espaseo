What is Spasmodic Dysphonia?

Spasmodic dysphonia is a neurological disorder that results in involuntary spasms of the muscles that control the vocal cords. This condition can lead to a strained or strangled voice, making communication difficult. Here are some key points about spasmodic dysphonia:

  • It is categorized into two main types: adductor spasmodic dysphonia, which causes the vocal cords to slam together, and abductor spasmodic dysphonia, where the vocal cords do not come together properly.
  • The exact cause is unknown, but it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors.
  • Symptoms can fluctuate, with some individuals experiencing periods of relative normalcy.
  • Treatment options include voice therapy and, in some cases, botulinum toxin injections to help relax the vocal cords.
